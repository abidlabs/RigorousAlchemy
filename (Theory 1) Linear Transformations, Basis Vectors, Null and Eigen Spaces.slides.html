A couple of facts about eigenvectors:¶

1. A vector in the null space of $T$ is also part of the eigen space of $T$¶

2. Symmetric matrices have a full set of of eigenvectors¶

Recap¶

1. Linear transformations satisfy two properties:¶

$$ T(\vec{u}) + T(\vec{v}) = T(\vec{u} + \vec{v}) $$¶

$$ T(s \cdot \vec{u}) = s \cdot T(\vec{u}) $$¶

2. Conservation of dimensions¶

$$ \textbf{dim}({\text{input space of } T}) = \textbf{dim}({\text{range of } T}) + \textbf{dim}({\text{null space of } T})$$¶

3. Eigenvectors are defined as:¶

$$T(\vec{u}) = s \cdot \vec{u}, \text{ where }\; \vec{u} \ne \vec{0}$$¶

Symmetric matrices have a full set of eigenvectors. In general, matrices may have fewer or none.¶
